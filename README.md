# Form Notation

[![DOI](https://zenodo.org/badge/1275671721.svg)](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20779731)

Form Notation is a visual music notation that encodes pitch, octave, and rhythm with geometric shapes, colors, and a circular duration indicator (the **halo**). It is designed to be read and written in minutes, and it round-trips with both MIDI and formal composition (traditional, staff-based notation).

## At a Glance

| Channel | Encodes | Vocabulary |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Shape** | Pitch class (which of the 12 chromatic notes) | 12 shapes |
| **Color** | Octave (register) | 11 colors |
| **Halo** | Duration | proportion of a circle |

A note is a shape (its pitch) in a color (its octave) beside a halo (its length). Chords are notes stacked at the same position. That is the whole system.

## Using Form

The notation itself is free for almost any use: building software and applications, integrating Form into any DAW or tool, designing original instruments, and producing your own educational materials, courses, and videos. You may also describe a product as "works with Form notation."

A small number of commercial fields are reserved (merchandise, entertainment including video games, formal school/college/university curriculum, and physical FormKey hardware). See **[BRAND-AND-TRADEMARK.md](BRAND-AND-TRADEMARK.md)** for the full, plain-language breakdown.

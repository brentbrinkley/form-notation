# Contributing to Form Notation

Thank you for your interest in Form Notation. This document explains how to propose changes to the standard.

## 1. How to Suggest a Change

- Open an **issue** to raise a problem, ambiguity, or idea.
- Suggestions are ideas and feedback only. For now, the specification text is authored solely by the BDFL, who writes any change that is adopted; outside text (pull requests of spec content) is not merged at this time.

Please keep suggestions focused on the standard itself. Implementation details (how a particular app, DAW, or instrument is built) belong in those projects, not in the specification.

## 2. Normative vs. Non-normative

- **Normative** changes affect the vocabulary, the duration set, the halo, the event model, or conformance. These require ratification by the BDFL and result in a new version.
- **Non-normative** changes (examples, wording, reference artwork, palette, recommendations) are lighter and may be accepted without a normative version bump.

State clearly which kind of change you are proposing.

## 3. Review and Ratification

Form Notation is stewarded under a Benevolent Dictator for Life model. **Brent Brinkley (BDFL)** has final say over what is accepted. Whether any suggestion is adopted is entirely at the BDFL's discretion: there is no obligation to respond to, act on, implement, or explain a decision about any suggestion, and no suggestion creates any expectation of inclusion. See [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md) for the full process.

## 4. Your Submissions

Suggestions are non-confidential. By submitting one, you agree that Polyhedra LLC may use, decline, or independently develop the same or similar ideas without restriction, attribution, or compensation, and that you acquire no rights in the standard by submitting. Because the specification text is authored by the BDFL, no outside text currently enters the standard, so there is nothing to license or assign. If the contribution model expands in the future to accept outside text, it will do so under a contributor agreement defined at that time. Submitting grants no rights to the **names** ("Form", "Form Notation", "FormKey") or the **reference glyph artwork**, which remain reserved (see [BRAND-AND-TRADEMARK.md](BRAND-AND-TRADEMARK.md)).

## 5. Scope

In scope: the notation's vocabulary, durations, halo, event model, layout rules, conformance, and the accompanying policy documents. Out of scope: how any specific product parses input, stores files, or implements its user interface.

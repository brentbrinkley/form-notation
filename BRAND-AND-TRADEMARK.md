# Form Notation Brand & Trademark Policy

This policy explains, in plain language, what you may do freely with Form Notation and what is reserved to Polyhedra LLC. The specification text is open (CC BY 4.0); this document governs the **name**, the **artwork**, and a small set of **commercial fields**.

## 1. Two Buckets

Everything falls into one of two buckets:

1. **The notation is open.** The shape, color, and duration system is free to use for almost any purpose.
2. **The brand and certain assets and fields are reserved.** The names, the reference glyph artwork, and a few commercial fields belong to Polyhedra LLC.

Reservation is enforced through **trademark** and **copyright on concrete assets**, never by restricting use of the notation itself.

## 2. Always Open

You may, without permission and commercially or not:

- Build software and applications around Form.
- Integrate Form into any DAW, plug-in, or other tool.
- Design original instruments that use Form.
- Produce your own educational materials, tutorials, courses, books, and videos, including independent and professional courses.
- Use the official Form glyph assets (published in `reference-glyphs/`) in your Form-compliant work, under the asset license in §4.
- Describe a product as "works with Form notation" so long as compliant with spec (a truthful, descriptive reference).

## 3. Reserved to Polyhedra LLC

The following are reserved. Use in these areas requires a **prior written license** from Polyhedra LLC, and is not permitted without one.

**Names and branding**

- "Form", "Form Notation", and "FormKey" as product names, branding, or product identity.
- The official Form logo and certification marks.

**Assets**

- **Copyright in the reference glyph artwork and palette** (© 2009–2026 Brent Brinkley / Polyhedra LLC). Ownership is retained by Polyhedra LLC; the artwork itself is **free to use under the asset license in §4** for Form-compliant, non-reserved purposes. Using the artwork *within* a reserved field requires a **prior written license** from Polyhedra LLC. The *shapes as concepts* are open either way.

**Commercial fields**

- **Merchandise** — branded goods, apparel, printed goods, toys, and games.
- **Entertainment** — branded entertainment properties, **including video games that incorporate Form**.
- **Curriculum** — the **formal curriculum of a school system, college, or university**. (Independent and professional courses, public tutorials, books, and videos remain open.)
- **Physical FormKey hardware** — building a physical FormKey, or a FormKey-type instrument that copies its design or identity. (Original instruments that merely use Form notation remain open.)

**Why these fields are reserved.** Each of these areas reflects the Form brand broadly and in public: it is how people encounter Form as an identity and an experience, not merely as a notation. Because these uses carry the brand's reputation into the public forum, they warrant a higher degree of reservation, so that the public face of Form stays coherent and trustworthy. The fields listed above are what is reserved; this reasoning states the principle behind them, and does not narrow the list.

## 4. Using the Form Glyph Assets

The official Form reference glyph set and palette are published in this repository (`reference-glyphs/`). Polyhedra LLC **retains copyright** in them and grants the following license.

You may use the official Form glyph assets, including in commercial products, provided **all** of the following hold:

1. **Compliant use.** Your use renders or supports Form notation in a Form-compliant way, as defined by the specification (including the normative halo geometry). The assets are for representing Form, not for unrelated purposes.
2. **Not a reserved field.** Your use is not within a reserved field (§3): merchandise, entertainment including video games, the formal curriculum of a school system, college, or university, or physical FormKey hardware.
3. **As published.** You use the glyphs as published. You may scale them and apply the spec's octave colors, but you may not alter a shape so that it misrepresents the glyph or breaks compliance.
4. **No false endorsement.** You do not use the assets to imply official endorsement or certification, or as your product's own brand identity. The names and logo remain reserved (§3).

This is a license, not a transfer: copyright stays with Polyhedra LLC, and the license does not extend to the reserved fields. Asset use within a reserved field requires a **prior written license** from Polyhedra LLC (see §7) and is not permitted without one. If you would rather not use the official assets, you may draw your own glyphs, provided each stays clearly recognizable as the named shape (see the specification, §6).

## 5. How Reservation Works

In each reserved field the value lives in the brand and the specific assets, not in the abstract idea. Polyhedra LLC protects these through trademark (the names and marks) and copyright (the artwork, materials, and product designs). The open notation is never restricted; what is reserved is the **official, branded, asset-backed** version of these fields.

## 6. "Form-compliant"

Any implementation that meets the conformance requirements of the specification (§15) may describe itself, truthfully and descriptively, as **Form-compliant**. Using the **name** or **logo** in product identity, or claiming official certification, requires a **prior written license** from Polyhedra LLC and is prohibited without one.

## 7. Commercial Licensing

Use within any reserved field, or of the names, logo, or certification marks, requires a **written license executed with Polyhedra LLC**. No such use is permitted until that license is granted in writing; informal approval, silence, or non-enforcement does not constitute a license. To request one, contact Polyhedra LLC.
